How does the talent acquisition process work?

The talent acquisition process works like this:

1. Workforce Planning: Identifying current and future hiring needs and establishing the skill sets required for specific roles.

2. Sourcing Candidates: Utilizing various channels like job boards, social media, networking, and recruitment agencies to attract potential candidates.

3. Screening and Selection: Reviewing resumes, conducting initial interviews, and assessing candidate qualifications to create a shortlist of top talent.

4. Interviewing: Conducting structured interviews to evaluate candidates’ skills, experience, and cultural fit with the organization.

5. Assessments and Testing: Employing additional tools like psychometric tests or technical assessments to further evaluate candidates’ capabilities.

6. Offer and Negotiation: Extending job offers to successful candidates and negotiating terms to ensure mutual agreement.

7. Onboarding: Integrating new hires into the organization through orientation, training, and mentorship programs, setting them up for success in their roles.

How can a manager recruit better?

A manager can recruit better by following the steps below.

  1. Start with No:
    • Look for reasons not to hire to stay objective and highlight risk elements.
  2. Continue with Attitude and Track Record:
    • Prioritize candidates with great attitudes who communicate well.
    • Analyze job types and durations on CVs for insights into a candidate’s adaptability and commitment.
  3. Don’t Heavily Rely on Interview and CV:
    • Use interviews and CVs as part of the decision-making process, not the only factors.
    • Craft detailed job descriptions focusing on the profile of a great hire.
    • Conduct non-traditional reference checks for more objective feedback.
  4. Look Within:
    • Analyze top performers within the organization to identify common traits and backgrounds.
    • Use these insights to create a profile of an ideal hire.

What’s the difference between recruiting, hiring and talent acquisition?

Recruiting, hiring, and talent acquisition differ in scope and focus within the employment process. Recruiting involves actively searching for potential candidates to fill immediate roles; hiring is the final step in choosing and onboarding a candidate for a specific position; and talent acquisition is a broader, ongoing strategy aimed at building a pipeline of skilled candidates for current and future needs.

A company might recruit and hire a software developer for a current project, while talent acquisition efforts would focus on establishing relationships with top tech talent to fulfill ongoing skill requirements. A 2021 report by Deloitte, Global Human Capital Trends, highlights that organizations with a long-term talent acquisition strategy see stronger workforce alignment and adaptability in response to changing needs compared to those focused solely on recruiting and hiring.

    Managers often prioritize technical skills over attitude, but hiring for attitude is more important. For example, if you need someone to start a podcast but don’t have a specific skill set in mind, hire a candidate who is a self-starter and has a strong desire to learn. It’s not about finding the perfect resume; it’s about hiring someone who grow, take initiative, and learn new skills independently.
  2. Throwing New Hires into the Deep End
    Entrepreneurs eager to delegate tasks to new hires, but it’s crucial not to overwhelm them from day one. New employees should be guided and shown the ropes, especially in the first days. This helps them build confidence and gain early wins. It’s essential to invest time in their success rather than just giving them tasks and walking away.
